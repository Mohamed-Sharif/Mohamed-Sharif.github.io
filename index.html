What I Did.

Over the course of my Bachelor's and my ITI journies, I have worked on many projects, that ranged in topic from software engineering to Energy systems and Mechanical Engineering.

  • Vortex Bladeless Wind Turbine (VBWT)

    Our team has successfully designed a groundbreaking wind turbine with the vision of ensuring universal access to stable energy. Through the development of an innovative home appliance device, we have tailored our solution specifically for efficient energy production in low wind velocities, which are prevalent in Egypt (less than 5 m/s). This cutting-edge technology empowers households to harness renewable energy, meeting their power needs reliably and sustainably."

    • L'Oréal Brandstorm 2020 project

      "As part of the L'Oréal challenge in 2020, I formed a team of three that focused on developing a sustainable packaging solution with a significantly reduced environmental footprint compared to plastics. Our approach involved creating a comprehensive business model centered around a novel combination of paper and bioplastics derived entirely from agricultural waste, such as corn and rice waste. This innovative and sustainable packaging method showcases our commitment to environmental stewardship and offers a viable alternative to traditional plastic packaging."

      • Crowd Funding App using Python and MongoDB

        I developed a Crowd Funding App using Python and MongoDB. This application allows users to register, create funding campaigns, and manage their own campaigns.

      • Social Media App using NodeJs

        I utilized Node.js to develop a robust Social Media Backend Server capable of enabling users to perform essential operations such as creating, reading, updating, and deleting posts, comments, and reviews. The server incorporates crucial security features including role-based authentication and data validation to ensure data integrity and user privacy. For efficient data storage and aggregation, I employed MongoDB as the underlying database technology.

      • Jenkins CI/CD Pipeline Setup for Node.js App with Docker, Terraform, Ansible, Slack Notifications

        I designed a Jenkins CI/CD pipeline for a Node.js application using Docker, Terraform, Ansible, Slack notifications, Audit Logs, and dynamic job scaling on EKS. By containerizing the Node.js application with Docker, I achieved portability and simplified deployment across different environments. Terraform was utilized to provision the required infrastructure as code. Ansible played a crucial role in configuration management, automating setup tasks and maintaining consistency across environments. Slack notifications were integrated to keep the development team informed of build status and deployment updates Audit Logs were implemented to track pipeline activities, providing detailed logging for troubleshooting. The Jenkins infrastructure was deployed on EKS, offering scalability and flexibility. With dynamic job scaling, the pipeline adjusted resources based on workload, optimizing efficiency.

      • Udacity Pick and Place Project: Robotics Perception, Planning, and Control

        I designed and completed the Udacity Pick and Place project, showcasing my expertise in perception, planning, and control algorithms. I programmed a robot arm to autonomously identify, grasp, and place objects using computer vision techniques. I implemented motion planning and control algorithms for efficient and precise manipulation. Throughout the project, I refined algorithms and optimized parameters to enhance performance. This project demonstrated my practical skills in robotics, including computer vision, motion planning, and control.

      • C++ Route Planner Project: Pathfinding and Optimization

        I coded a Route Planner in C++ where I implemented pathfinding and optimization algorithms. The goal of the project was to develop a program that finds the shortest path between two points on a map and optimizes the route. I implemented the A* search algorithm to find the shortest path and applied a heuristic-based optimization to reorder waypoints, ensuring efficient route planning. Additionally, I incorporated error handling and input validation to handle invalid or inaccessible locations. Overall, the project demonstrated my ability to design and implement graph-based algorithms, apply pathfinding techniques, and optimize routes. It showcased my proficiency in data structures, algorithms, and problem-solving skills.
